Something about coffee

Type of Roast

The history of coffee roasting dates back to the 15th century, with the earliest recorded roasting method being in Arabia, where coffee beans were roasted over an open fire. Light roasts became popular in Europe in the 19th century, while dark roasts gained popularity in America in the 20th century.

Coffee grown worldwide can trace its heritage back centuries to the ancient coffee forests on the Ethiopian plateau. There, legend says the goat herder Kaldi first discovered the potential of these beloved beans.

There's something special about a freshly brewed cup of coffee, especially when it's made using our dip coffee brewing method. This method involves steeping freshly ground coffee in hot water for several minutes before slowly plunging the filter down to extract every last drop of flavor. The result is a rich, full-bodied cup of coffee that's smooth and delicious from start to finish. With every sip, you'll taste the subtle notes of the coffee's origin, whether it's a bold African blend or a milder Central American roast. It's the perfect way to start your morning or enjoy a mid-day pick-me-up.
